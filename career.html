Career Journey of MS Dhoni

Early Career

Before entering cricket, Dhoni worked as a Train Ticket Examiner at Kharagpur railway station. His cricket journey started with small club matches and domestic tournaments like the Ranji Trophy for Bihar (1999-2000 season). His fearless batting caught the selectors’ eyes, paving his way to the national stage.

International Debut

Dhoni debuted for India in December 2004 against Bangladesh. His breakthrough came in 2005 when he smashed 148 runs against Pakistan at Visakhapatnam — the world first saw the explosive Dhoni. His aggressive style changed how wicketkeeper-batsmen were perceived.

Captaincy & Legacy

In 2007, Dhoni led a young Indian team to victory in the inaugural T20 World Cup. He later captained India to win the 2011 Cricket World Cup and the 2013 Champions Trophy. Known for his cool mind under pressure, Dhoni redefined Indian cricket leadership before retiring from international cricket in 2020.
